Quasi-connectivity

Quasi-connectivity is a special property of three blocks: pistons, droppers, and dispensers.

In short, any one of these blocks (which we'll call QC-able blocks) can be powered from one block above where they usually would. If the block above them would be powered, they'll be powered too.

However, the weirdness arises when you account for block updates. QC was not an intentional behaviour when it was first introduced, so block update ranges don't account for QC. As a result, QC-able blocks may be powered but fail to realise it, until they're updated by some other source. This also applies to depowering.
